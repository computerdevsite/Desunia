What Are Viruses?

A virus is a type of malicious software that attaches itself to a legitimate program or file to spread and infect other systems. Once executed, viruses replicate and spread to other devices, often corrupting data, stealing information, or damaging files. Viruses can be spread through email attachments, infected websites, or file-sharing programs.

What Is Malware?

Malware is an umbrella term for malicious software, including viruses, worms, Trojans, ransomware, and spyware. Malware is designed to infiltrate and harm devices, steal information, or disrupt systems. It can spread through vulnerabilities in software or by tricking users into downloading harmful files or clicking malicious links.

What Is Spam?

Spam refers to unwanted and unsolicited messages, primarily through email. Spam can include advertisements, phishing attempts, or malicious links leading to malware infections. The main goal of spam is often to get the recipient to click on a harmful link or share personal information.

How Antivirus Software Protects You

Antivirus software is crucial for defending against viruses, malware, and spam. It works by scanning files and programs for malicious activity. Most modern antivirus solutions include real-time protection, automatic updates, and spam filters to keep your system secure. These tools help identify, block, and remove harmful software before it can cause damage.

Antivirus software can protect against:

  • Known viruses and malware
  • Phishing and spam emails
  • Ransomware attacks
  • Spyware and tracking threats
